Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • The novel opens as the protagonist, Holden Caulfield, begins relaying what has brought him to therapy.
  • Holden however shies away from his early life, choosing to focus on more recent occurrences.
  • He does however bring up his brother, D.B., briefly as he explains that he sold out his writing talents for the glamour of Hollywood.
  • Holden then dives into the details of what has brought him into therapy.
  • Holden had been attending a very prestigious and well-to-do prep school known as Pencey Prep in Agerstown, Pennsylvania.
  • He goes on to explain that at the end of the recent fall term he was told he could not return to Pencey Prep, as he had failed all of his classes except for English.
  • It is clear though that his failure does not stem from a lack of intelligence, instead a lack of motivation seems the reason.
  • Holden describes the last Saturday at Pencey before he was set to leave for Christmas vacation.
  • On Pencey's campus a large hill overlooks the school's football field, this particular Saturday the school's team is playing an important game against the bitter rivals from Saxon Hall.
  • Holden has no interest in football, and would never have stopped by to see the game if it weren't for an embarrassing mistake he made earlier in the day.
  • Holden, the manager of the fencing team, had been in charge of all of the the gear for a meet in New York City earlier in the day, however he managed to lose it all on the subway forcing the team to return to school.
  • While overlooking the field Holden contemplates his time spent at Pencey Prep, and though most of his experiences were negative, he does seem to have some fond memories.
  • Deciding to take advantage of his time left, Holden runs across campus to the home of his favorite teacher Mr. Spencer.
  • Mr. Spencer was Holden's history teacher a few years back, and recently has become very ill.
  • When Holden arrives at his home he is very winded from his habit of smoking; Mr. Spencer's wife greets him and he enters to say his goodbyes.
  • Chapter 2
  • Holden seems to harbor a great respect for his former teacher, and despite his deteriorating condition, Holden is not put off.
  • The two begin a conversation, however it swiftly turns into a lecture as Mr. Spencer is ashamed at how Holden can go through life without any care, particularly when it comes to his academics.
  • It is quite clear that the lecture is intended for Holden's best interests, however Holden is obviously annoyed and proceeds to cut the conversation short and leave the Spencers'
  • Chapter 3
  • Holden returns to his dorm room in Ossenburger Hall, named for a famous graduate of the school who made a vast fortune in the funeral business.
  • Holden begins to relax and read from the novel Out of Africa, however he is interrupted after a short while by a student who lives next door.
  • The student is referred to as Ackley, and by Holden's description; he is quite a repulsive teenager.
  • Ackley is described as being extremely pimply, with constant halitosis (bad breath).
  • Ackley is also a very nosy person and constantly invades Holden's personal space.
  • While in Holden's room Ackley proceeds to badger Holden with questions and irritate him to the point where Holden is near snapping when Holden's roommate Stradlater joins them.
  • Stradlater is a very precocious and handsome teenager who seems to have no trouble with the girls at Pencey Prep.
  • It is obvious that Stradlater intimidates Ackley, and he immediately leaves the room when Stradlater enters.
  • Stradlater announces to Holden that he has returned to shave before a date that he has that evening.
  • Chapter 4
  • Holden decides to follow Stradlater to the bathroom when he goes to shave so he can have what is probably the last conversation with his roommate.
  • While in the bathroom Holden inwardly compares Ackley and Stradlater.
  • He remarks that on the outside Ackley is a very disgusting person, and meanwhile Stradlater is very similar, however he does a good job keeping his disgusting nature hidden from those outside the dorms, particularly girls.
  • Holden refers to Stradlater as a secret slob.
  • While in the bathroom Stradlater asks Holden to write an English essay for him.
  • Stradlater explains that due to the girl he is seeing he has very little time for his studies.
  • The girl is Jane Gallagher, however Stradlater is unaware and refers to her as Jean.
  • This upsets Holden as he is well aware who the girl is and has deep feelings for her.
  • Holden cannot stand the fact that someone such as Stradlater will be taking her on a date as he obviously is only interested in her sexual aspects above anything else.
  • Eventually Stradlater is ready to leave, he borrows Holden's favorite hound's-tooth jacket and sets out.
  • Meanwhile Ackley returns to Holden's room and infuriates him even more for the next hour before dinner.
  • Chapter 5
  • The dinner proves to be very dissatisfying, and following dinner Holden enjoys a snowball fight with some of the other boys.
  • He then meets up with a friend, Mal Brossard, and the two plan to head into Agerstown.
  • Holden, feeling sympathetic for Ackley, decides to bring him along.
  • The boys plan to see a movie, however it turns out that both Mal and Ackley have already seen the only movie playing, so instead the boys grab a burger and visit an arcade before returning to school.
  • Back at school Mal heads off looking for a chance to gamble with the other boys while Ackley and Holden return to Holden's room.
  • Ackley proceeds to pop his pimples on Holden's bed and tell of a girl he once slept with, however Holden has no interest as he does not believe the story.
  • Eventually Holden is able to convince Ackley to leave by explaining that he has to work on an English essay.
  • Holden then begins the essay for Stradlater with the simple instructions that it can be on anything simple in nature such as a house.
  • Holden however struggles with this topic and instead decides to write about a baseball glove his younger brother Allie used to play with.
  • Allie died of leukemia several years earlier, however when he was alive he would always carry around an old baseball glove on which he would write poems in green ink.
  • Holden begins to recall his memories of Allie and explains that he was the best person he knew, he was a very thoughtful and caring person, especially for his young age.
  • Holden was broken by his death and carries it to this day.
  • Chapter 6
  • Much later that night Stradlater returns expecting his assignment to be finished.
  • He takes it from Holden and begins to read it, however he is displeased to find it does not particularly match the prompt.
  • Holden is enraged by Stradlater's reaction to the essay and proceeds to tear it to pieces.
  • Still upset, Holden decides to smoke a cigarette in the room to bother Stradlater as he pesters him with questions about the date.
  • Stradlater however is unwilling to share details, which further upsets Holden to the point that he attacks Stradlater.
  • Holden becomes so aggressive that Stradlater is driven to punch him, and in doing so bloodies Holden's nose.
  • Afraid he has gone to far, Stradlater leaves the room and Holden leaves to Ackley's room where he looks at his face.
  • Chapter 7
  • While in Ackley's room Holden tries to calm himself with meaningless conversation, however it only annoys both of them.
  • After failed attempts to fall asleep in Ackley's roommate's bed, Holden proceeds to try again to have a conversation with Ackley, however Ackley is not interested causing Holden to leave on account of Ackley being 'phony'.
  • Holden returns to his room and devises a plan.
  • He will leave for New York that night and stay a few extra days there before returning to his parents' home.
  • He packs his things, puts on his hunting hat, and heads into the hallway.
  • He then proceeds to shout into the hall "Sleep tight, ya morons" before beginning his journey to New York.
  • Chapter 8
  • Holden heads to the nearby train station where he catches the last train to New York.
  • While at a stop in Trenton an older woman enters the train and sits near Holden.
  • Holden learns that she is the mother of one of Holden's classmates.
  • Though Holden despises her son, he chooses to amuse himself by telling lies to his mother.
  • Holden tells her that her son, Ernest, is extremely popular and modest which pleases the woman greatly.
  • She proceeds to question Holden, and he tells her that his name is Rudolph Schmidt, however in fact that is the name of the janitor.
  • Holden explains that he has left school early for an operation in New York.
  • Chapter 9
  • Holden arrives at Penn Station in New York and plans on calling someone, however decides against it after he realizes that his brother and sister would be no help to him, and that anyone else he could think of calling would not want to hear from him.
  • Instead he hails a cab which takes him to the Edmont Hotel in downtown New York.
  • Once at the hotel Holden pays for a room.
  • His room is very high up and looks across a courtyard to other hotel rooms.
  • Holden peers at the other rooms and discovers many lewd and promiscuous acts taking place.
  • The view arouses Holden and he decides to call an easy girl that he knows lives in New York.
  • The girl is named Faith Cavendish, however when she answers she tells him that it is far too late and proposes they meet the next day.
  • Holden is disappointed however and decides to hang up on her rather than make plans.
  • Chapter 10
  • Holden decides to head downstairs to a nightclub located near the lobby of the hotel, however before doing so he contemplates calling his younger sister Phoebe.
  • He however decides against it, but does provide the reader some information about his sister.
  • Holden describes her as being very similar to his dead brother Allie, he explains that she is a very intelligent girl for her age and discusses his joyful experiences attending the cinema with her.
  • He then shifts away from her description to the present as he heads downstairs to the club.
  • While in the club Holden pretends he is much older than his current age and tries to order alcohol, however he is denied, as the bartender is not deceived.
  • Holden then proceeds to spend about an hour flirting with a group of girls he has met from Seattle.
  • The girls are however slightly amused with Holden as it is obvious he is much younger than them.
  • It turns out nothing comes of the encounter as Holden ends up paying for their drinks and leaving.
  • Chapter 11
  • Outside of the club Holden proceeds to inform the reader of his relationship with Jane Gallagher, he explains that they met a few summers ago in Maine.
  • They lived next to each other and became very close spending every day during that summer together.
  • He adds that their relationship was not very physical except for one day when she was mistreated by her stepfather.
  • That day Holden proceeded to comfort her by holding and kissing her, however after that nothing else ever materialized besides holding hands.
  • It is clear that the two were not much more than friends, however Holden confined in her more than he had to anyone else, even showing her Allie's baseball glove.
  • Holden soon becomes upset with his thoughts about Jane as he realizes that he is so far away from that wonderful summer.
  • He returns to his room, however has trouble sleeping and decides to again go downstairs, this time hailing a cab.
  • Chapter 12
  • Holden finds a cab that is driven by a man named Horowitz.
  • He asks Horowitz to take him to Greenwich Village to a night club he used to frequent with his older brother.
  • During the drive Holden badgers Horowitz with questions, however a question about the ducks in central park seems to upset Horowitz and the two do not speak for the rest of the ride.
  • He arrives at the club known as Ernie's and proceeds to buy a drink and absorb the atmosphere in the club.
  • After a short while Holden runs into a girl that his brother used to date, however he is so annoyed by her he leaves shortly there after.
  • Chapter 13
  • Outside the club Holden becomes embarrassed with himself for leaving the club after taking the long journey to Ernie's.
  • Too ashamed to hail another cab, Holden decides to walk the 41 blocks from the club back to the hotel.
  • During the long walk Holden thinks back to his time at Pencey and recalls a time when someone stole a pair of his favorite gloves.
  • Holden thinks to himself about what he would have done to the thief, however he then remarks to the reader that he probably would have done nothing as he is a coward.
  • It is quite clear that Holden struggles with his self image.
  • He arrives at the hotel and takes the elevator up stairs; on the elevator he speaks with the elevator operator who offers to find Holden a prostitute.
  • Holden agrees and heads to his room to wait for the prostitute, while in solitude he thinks that his cowardly personality is the reason why he has yet to sleep with someone.
  • Eventually the prostitute arrives, and appears a lot younger and more innocent that Holden imagined.
  • Sunny, the prostitute, attempts to seduce Holden, however he cannot seem to go through with it.
  • He then explains that he cannot have sex because he has just had an operation prompting Sunny to ask for her money.
  • Holden gives her five dollars like he had agreed upon with the elevator operator, however she demands ten, yet he does not oblige her before she leaves.
  • Chapter 14
  • Again alone in the room Holden begins to chain smoke as he thinks about his dead brother Allie.
  • He recalls a time shortly before Allie died where Holden left him out of a game with BB-guns.
  • Holden is upset with himself for this, and decides that he should pray and go to sleep.
  • However he fails to say any prayers as he struggles with organized religion.
  • While laying in bed Holden is shocked as he hears a knock on the door; it is the elevator operator and Sunny demanding more money.
  • Holden refuses, however the elevator operator pins him to the wall and Sunny forcibly takes the money.
  • Holden attempts to insult the two, yet the operator wants nothing with it and proceeds to punch Holden in the stomach and walk out.
  • He finally returns to bed and goes to sleep.
  • Chapter 15
  • Holden awakes the next morning and decides to call Sally Hayes again and make a date.
  • He decides to leave the hotel and put his things in a locker in Grand Central Station before heading out into the city.
  • Holden thinks to himself about how he must be careful with his money as it upsets his father when he loses things.
  • He then mentions his mother for the first time, he explains that she was left very weak by Allie's death and he is afraid that his expulsion will only make her worse.
  • Holden then makes his way to a small restaurant where he has breakfast and strikes up a conversation with two nuns.
  • Holden is very friendly and interested with what the nuns have to say despite his previous statement that he cannot stand organized religion.
  • When the nuns leave he gives them ten dollars as a donation.
  • Chapter 16
  • Holden then makes his way back out in the city searching for a gift for his sister.
  • While walking he thinks about the visit he had with the nuns and is amazed at how selfless they are.
  • He eventually comes across a shop on Broadway where he finds a record for his sister, the record is called "Little Shirley Beans" and consists of several children's songs sung by a blues singer.
  • Holden enjoys his walk and is cheered by the sight of a young boy singing.
  • He thinks of his sister and cannot wait to see her, his better mood also convinces him to call Jane, however he hangs up when her mother answers the phone.
  • Holden then decides to buy tickets for later that day at a theater so he has somewhere to take Sally.
  • Following this Holden heads to Central Park where he hopes he will come across his sister who usually spends her Sundays rollerblading in the park, however he is not lucky enough to catch her, only a friend.
  • Phoebe's friend in the park explains that Phoebe might be on a school trip to the Natural History Museum.
  • Holden decides to head towards the Museum despite knowing for sure, on his walk he contemplates all his experiences going to the museum as a child, and how no matter how much he had changed in between visits, the museum remained the same.
  • Chapter 17
  • Holden heads from the museum to the Biltmore Hotel where he is too meet Sally at two for their date, however he is forced to wait for her to arrive.
  • Eventually when she does he forgets all his annoyance with her for being late as she shows up looking particularly attractive.
  • The two then take a taxi back towards Broadway and the theatre, on the way the two make out in the back.
  • The play bothers Holden greatly as he views the actors as being cocky and tryhards, he is therefore relieved greatly by the intermission until Sally begins to flirt with another boy causing Holden to show the first signs that he is falling apart.
  • Following the play Holden then takes Sally ice-skating, and she wears a short skirt.
  • It is quite obvious that she is out to impress others, however Holden doesn't seem to mind as he believes it looks good.
  • The two then decide to head to an indoor spot to talk after skating, however Holden makes things awkward by going on a great rant about people he refers to as 'phonies'.
  • He begins to scheme up a grand plan about running away with Sally, however she is in no way amused by this causing Holden to become extremely upset to the point of demeaning Sally.
  • He quickly apologizes, yet the date is already ruined and the two part their separate ways.
  • Chapter 18
  • Holden heads out into the city again, this time contemplating Jane again.
  • He thinks to an experience at a dance a few years back where Jane was dancing with a boy who appeared to Holden as quite the pretentious prick, however Jane explained that he was much the opposite and really self-conscious.
  • Holden viewed this explanation as an excuse for Jane to date guys like that.
  • His thoughts on this experience prompt him to call her once more, however this time no one answers.
  • He then decides to call an old friend, Carl Luce, and make plans to meet up for drinks that evening.
  • Holden still has a few hours before the meeting and choses to head to Radio City Music Hall to see a movie, however before the movie he has to sit through the Rockettes' Christmas show.
  • He sits through the show brooding over the Rockettes' and how 'phony' they are.
  • He does however have a fond memory of his brother Allie during the show, the two would go see shows at Radio City just to watch one of the drummers in the orchestra.
  • Though he was a very small part, he seemed to enjoy his job more than anyone else, Holden admired this trait.
  • The movie eventually begins and ends and Holden leaves for his meeting with Luce, on the walk to the bar he thinks about the army and realizes that he could never enlist, he would rather be shot than go to war.
  • Chapter 19
  • Holden arrives at the bar to meet Luce and recalls how when the two went to the Whooton School, Luce would enlighten the younger boys about sex.
  • Luce is three years older than Holden, and it is clear when they meet up that he has matured a lot since graduating high school and attending Columbia.
  • Holden tries to draw Luce into a conversation about sex just like in high school, however Luce has no interest.
  • He even suggests that perhaps a psychiatrist should check out Holden.
  • Holden remembers that Luce's father is one, and makes a rude comment wondering whether his father has ever checked out Luce.
  • Luce is however not amused and decides to leave.
  • Chapter 20
  • Alone at the bar, Holden begins to get belligerently drunk and make a fool of himself.
  • He first calls Sally waking her and her grandmother and angering both, after this he proceeds to miserably attempt to pick up two girls at the bar but is quite obviously unsuccessful.
  • Holden leaves the bar and heads towards the duck pond in central park thinking about Allie and Phoebe.
  • On the way he drops the record he bought for his sister, breaking it.
  • Furious with himself, Holden is once again near breaking.
  • He thinks back to Allie's death and is so upset he decides heading home might be his best option.
  • He plans on sneaking in, speaking with Phoebe, and leaving again.
  • Chapter 21
  • Holden eventually makes the long walk back to his family's apartment and takes the elevator upstairs to their floor where he then sneaks in.
  • He heads to Phoebe's room where his surprised not to find his sister, he then heads to D.B.'s old room where Phoebe is fast asleep.
  • Holden spends some time just watching her sleep as he peruses her schoolbooks and enjoys reading her notes.
  • Eventually he wakes her up and she is so excited she is on the verge of shouting.
  • In the next few minutes she proceeds to tell Holden as much as she can about what has been going on in her life until she suddenly becomes quiet.
  • She realizes that Holden has been expelled from school as he is home early.
  • She is frightened, as she fears that their father will punish Holden and she covers herself in her blankets and quits speaking.
  • Holden heads out to buy cigarettes.
  • Chapter 22
  • After buying cigarettes Holden returns and tries to explain himself to Phoebe.
  • He tells her his reasoning for why he failed out of school and expresses his contempt for his classes and 'phonies'.
  • This does not however help his cause as Phoebe is still upset and argues that Holden harbors contempt for almost everything, she even challenges him to name something he likes.
  • He struggles to do so and thinks back to his days at Elkton Hills, he recalls a boy who killed himself after being harassed by other students, the boy was named James Castle.
  • Eventually Holden can only come up with Allie to which Phoebe responds by saying he is dead, she once more tries to get through by asking what Holden wants to do with his future, however he can only provide a confusing quote.
  • "If a body catch a body comin' through the rye".
  • He explains that he wants to be able to help children that come to close to falling off a cliff, to be as he puts it 'the catcher in the rye'.
  • She retorts by saying he has heard the lyric wrong and his statement makes no sense.
  • Chapter 23
  • Holden leaves the room to make a phone call to an old teacher, Mr. Antolini who lives in the city.
  • Mr. Antolini offers Holden a place to stay for the evening after learning that Holden has been kicked out of another school.
  • Holden takes this opportunity to go back to James Castle's death and explains that Mr. Antolini was the only person to go up to his dead body and the only person to be respectful and strong in the situation.
  • Following the phone call Holden heads back into Phoebe's room and asks her to dance, the two enjoy a few songs before they hear the front door open and Holden must hide.
  • He hides in the closet as his mother enters to say goodnight to Phoebe.
  • After she leaves Holden says his goodbyes to Phoebe, explains he is going to travel out west, gives her his hunting hat, and leaves with the Christmas money Phoebe had been saving.
  • Chapter 24
  • Holden eventually arrives at Mr. Antolini's and finds the place quite the mess from an earlier dinner party.
  • Mr. Antolini is intoxicated, but nonetheless is quite welcoming.
  • The two have a conversation about his expulsion and Holden explains that he didn't like the rules and people at Pencey Prep.
  • Mr. Antolini begins to lecture him on the importance of schooling and accepting your role as a student.
  • However before the conversation becomes heated the two are interrupted by Mrs. Antolini who brings the two coffee.
  • The two then pick up the conversation and Mr. Antolini expresses his worry for Holden, he explains that he sees him heading down a path that will ultimately lead to his downfall.
  • He explains that many men have gone down this path, yet adds that it is possible to still turn it around, accept the importance of schooling and change his life for the better.
  • Eventually the conversation ends and Holden falls asleep on the couch.
  • He is surprisingly awoken by Mr. Antolini stroking his hair causing Holden to jump.
  • He accuses him of making an advance on him and promptly leaves.
  • Chapter 25
  • Holden spends the rest of the night sleeping in Grand Central Station.
  • The following day he wakes and begins walking all over Fifth Avenue in the city and it is obvious he is not in the greatest mental state as he constantly calls out to his dead brother to protect him.
  • Eventually he decides to leave the city and head west, however he wants to see Phoebe first so he stops off at her school and leaves a note for her telling her to meet him at the museum.
  • While at Phoebe's school he sees the words "fuck you" written on a wall and becomes even more pessimistic again.
  • He heads to the museum to wait for Phoebe yet is further frustrated when he sees the words 'Fuck you' again written on an exhibit.
  • Still waiting for Phoebe, Holden passes out briefly yet makes light of it.
  • Eventually she arrives carrying a suitcase and begins to beg Holden to bring him with her, however he is adamant against it.
  • Upset, she returns his hunting hat and threatens to leave prompting Holden to give up his desires to run away.
  • He calms her by offering to take her to the zoo, however she is still not over their argument.
  • Eventually however the two begin to reconcile and Holden takes her to the carousel and convinces her to ride it.
  • He sits on a park bench and watches her have fun and for the first time in years he is truly happy.
  • Chapter 26
  • The story wraps up there and Holden goes on to explain that following that day he returned home and became sick and was sent to a rest home.
  • He adds that he plans to go to a new school in the fall, and plans to work harder this time.
  • He finishes by explaining that telling his story was very difficult because it made him realize how much he misses the people close to him.
