Characters

  • Holden Caulfield
  • Holden Caulfield is the sixteen-year-old protagonist of the novel.
  • At the beginning of the novel, Holden has just been expelled from the prep school he attended, Pencey Prep, for failing grades as a junior.
  • Holden is a very articulate and intelligent person, however his struggles in school seem to stem from his lack of connection with adult society and his view of the adult world as phony.
  • Therefore he has no desire to succeed in his education as he views it as the final step in his childhood.
  • Despite Holden's disconnect with others, especially adults, it appears as if he has an emotional disconnect with himself as he struggles to understand his place as a person.
  • Ackley
  • Ackley lives next door to Holden in the dorms at Pencey Prep.
  • Holden despises Ackley greatly for his violations of Holden's personal space, his lack of hygiene, and his incessant lying about his sexual experiences.
  • Stradlater
  • Stradlater is Holden's roommate, which Holden secretly envies.
  • Stradlater is described as a very attractive boy who seems to be gifted with great charisma and a way with the girls at the prep school.
  • Holden remarks that despite Stradlater's outward appearance of cleanliness, he is secretly a slob, as the stuff he keeps private in his room are always messy.
  • Jane Gallagher
  • Jane never appears in the novel, however her character is very important to Holden.
  • One summer, Jane and Holden lived next door to each other in Maine, and the two became very close.
  • Holden holds a deep respect for and an attraction to her.
  • Phoebe Caulfield
  • Phoebe is Holden's younger sister who is about 10 years old.
  • Despite the age gap, Holden is very close with her as he feels she understands and connects with him.
  • She is a very pretty and intelligent young girl and is also far more mature than she appears.
  • Allie Caulfield
  • Allie was Holden's younger brother, however he died of leukemia a few years before the novel takes place.
  • Holden harbors a great love for his deceased brother, and holds onto a baseball glove of Allie's on which Allie wrote poems.
  • D. B. Caulfield
  • D.B. is Holden's older brother, he writes movie scripts in Hollywood.
  • Holden views the job working for Hollywood as a way that D.B. is selling out, as he believes he is a great author that should not limit himself.
  • Holden holds onto a collection of short stories that D.B. wrote as he loves the stories.
  • Sally Hayes
  • Sally is a teenage girl who Holden has been close with and dated off and on for many years.
  • She appears to be very smart and well read, however Holden claims that she is stupid, however it is not quite clear if this belief has to do with how he views her sexually.
  • Carl Luce
  • Luce is about three years older than Holden, and was Holden's advisor when he attended Whooton School before Pencey Prep.
  • Holden admired Luce for his sexual prowess and often had Luce tell him stories about his experiences.
  • Mr. Antolini
  • Mr. Antolini is a former teacher of Holden's at the Elkton Hills School.
  • Holden had a great appreciation for Mr. Antolini as a teacher and looks up to him.
  • He is a heavy drinker, and at the time of the novel he works at New York University.
  • Mr. Spencer
  • Mr. Spencer is Holden's current history teacher.
  • He recognizes Holden's potential and tries to urge Holden to work harder.
  • Maurice
  • He works as an elevator operator for the Edmont Hotel where Holden stays after leaving school.
  • Maurice helps Holden find a prostitute.
  • Sunny
  • Sunny is a prostitute who Holden hires in hopes of sleeping with.
  • He cannot go through with it, and instead spends their time together trying to connect to her.
